Frequently Asked Questions and Tips

Who is allowed to go to Batanes?

Only travelers who are fully vaccinated may enter Batanes for leisure purposes. Those ineligible for vaccination need to present proof of ineligibility like a medical certification or a birth certificate for children below 5 years old.

What are the entry points in Batanes?

The main entry point in Batanes is Basco Airport, located in the province’s capital. Most Philippine flights booked online to Basco Airport are from Manila.

What travel requirements do you need to prepare?

    Here are the requirements that you need to prepare before going to Batanes:

  1. Get a vaccination card or vaccination certificate (for fully-vaccinated)
  2. You need to show proof of your fully vaccinated status, and the Batanes local government unit accepts a valid vaccination card or a vaccination certificate from vaxcert.doh.gov.ph.
  3. Secure proof of ineligibility for a COVID-19 vaccination (for unvaccinated)
  4. Parents or guardians of children below 5 years old need to present the birth certificate of their child to prove ineligibility. Those with medical conditions need to present a medical certificate.
  5. Book accommodations and tours with DOT-accredited establishments
  6. Prior reservation with any of the DOT-accredited Batanes hotels and resorts is a must, as well as booking with DOT-accredited tour operators. Do-it-yourself tours are not allowed. It's recommended to book airport transfers as well for a hassle-free arrival and departure.

What are the guidelines and protocols to follow when you're in Batanes?

All residents and tourists must wear face masks, wash hands regularly, and practice proper social distancing, especially in public establishments, to help reduce the risk of community transmission of COVID-19 disease.

Arrival Protocols

All tourists will undergo health screening upon arrival. Those exhibiting symptoms will be tested for free, and those who test positive for COVID-19 will be isolated in a government facility.

Tour protocols

Tourists are strongly discouraged from mingling with locals who are not part of their Batanes itinerary.

What activities can you do in Batanes during quarantine?

While in the province, don’t miss the chance to visit top Batanes tourist spots, including its famous lighthouses and rolling hills.

When is the best time to go to Batanes?

The best time to visit Batanes is during the dry or summer season. In the Philippines, the months of March, April, May, and June are considered the hottest months with minimal rainfall. These are also the best months to visit Batanes. Fewer chances of rainfall mean fewer chances of delayed or canceled flights and you’ll be able to enjoy your trip better since Batanes tourist attractions are mostly outdoors.

What is the Climate and Weather in Batanes?

Batanes has a tropical climate like the rest of the Philippines. The average yearly temperature is 26.0 °C. April is the driest month, while August is the rainiest month so best to avoid traveling to Batanes during that time. November to February are the coldest and often have rainy days as well.

What to Wear?

During the dry summer months, wear comfortable clothes with breathable fabric since you’ll be spending most of the time outdoors. Better bring your sunblock and umbrella to keep you shaded from the sun. If you’re planning a trip in the rainy or cold months, bring an umbrella and raincoat/poncho to keep you dry. You might also want to bring extra clothes for a quick change during your Batanes tours.

How to go to Batanes?

The best way to go to Batanes is by direct flights or connecting flights to Basco. Depending on where you’re flying from, domestic flights to Basco can take between 70 and 110 minutes. If you’re in Metro Manila, Philippine Airlines (PAL) flies daily from Manila to Basco, Batanes for 1 hour and 45 minutes.

Try booking weekday flights instead of a weekend one (or long weekends in the Philippines) if you’re on a budget and looking for less expensive plane tickets. Normally, flights during these days are less expensive and one of the most recommended cheapest ways to Batanes.

How to get around Batanes?

By jeepney. They journey around the island of Batan from 5:00AM to 5:00PM (you often just need to flag them down at certain areas). If in doubt, feel free to talk to a local.

By tricycle. You can arrange this with your hotel, a tour operator, or directly with a tricycle driver. We call them as “Batoda” and apart from taking you to and from near distances, they can also provide day rentals to help you explore the island. To go around the north of Batan, they customarily ask Php 1,000, and for the south, about Php 1,500. (In Sabtang, touring the whole island starts at a cost of Php 800).

By motorcycle or bike. You can rent a motorcycle starting at Php 500 a day (it can go up if you want a guide/driver, if you want a bigger motorbike, etc. and if you want per hour, it can be about Php 150 per hour). A simple bike would cost about Php 300 a day (or Php 25 per hour). If in doubt about where to rent, just ask a local and they will direct you to the right place.

By car. If you’re coming with a big group, it’s best to do a tour for a hassle-free experience.

By boat. To get to the other inhabited islands of Sabtang and Itbayat, you will need to go through an exciting boat ride with our local boats called ‘faluwa’.

What to pack?

Make sure you are prepared to travel simply. Bring the bare necessities with you. Remember that you’ll be doing a lot of walking and boat rides so pack light. While bottled water is readily available in most convenient stores, be kind to Mother Earth (and your wallet). Bring your own water bottle and just refill from your hotel or homestay. This way, you also don’t have to carry empty water bottles around.

Is there a good internet connection in Batanes?

Given that Batanes is a far-flung group of islands, the internet connection there is quite limited and slow. Some hotels have it but they will often ask for an extra charge to use it; if you don’t want that, you can buy a Smart or Globe SIM card but they will only have LTE signal strength (alternatively, you can get an eSIM). There are also some computer cafes found in Basco with a decent internet connection, primarily used by the younger ones for gaming — but still, those are worth a shot.

Currency

Bring enough cash in Philippine Peso (PHP). There are only 2 ATM in Basco, Batanes and there are no foreign exchange facilities in town. Commercial establishments also do not accept credit card payments, so it’s best to bring cash and be smart with your expenses.

Language

Locals speak Ivatan, but the majority can speak and understand Filipino and English. Learning to speak the native tongue is a great way to befriend locals. Phrases like dius mamahes (thank you), ara ka mangu (how are you?), and avek dana / mangay aku na (goodbye) can go a long way.

Official Government

Batanes is headed by Provincial Governor Marilou Cayco and Vice Governor Ignacio Villa. The Batanes Provincial Board, also known as the Sangguniang Panlalawigan, serves as its provincial legislature. The presidents of the provincial chapters of the Association of Barangay Captains, the Councilors' League, and the provincial president of Sangguniang Kabataan are also ex officio members.
